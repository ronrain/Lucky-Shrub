Our Top Shrubs

Here at Lucky Shrub, we possess some of the most unique and beautiful shrubs around. Our experts and scientist have studied shrubs for decades and have published a plefora of books about shrubs and found some to have better qualities than others. Although some shrubs may not be on here or are labeled as being particularly special, that doesn't mean they're beautiful or unique. Here are some of the shrubs:

Long Lasting Blooms: Ninebark

Ninebark is an indigenous shrub that boasts a multitude of appealing attributes. Its vibrant foliage remains dark burgundy, chartreuse, or bronze throughout the entire season. Fragrant white blossoms adorn it during the middle to late spring period, and its naturally arching form eliminates the necessity for trimming. For more compact gardens, consider seeking out new dwarf varieties.

This shrub maintains its aesthetic appeal throughout most of the year and is a versatile addition to mixed borders, beds, or as a standout focal plant. Additionally, it attracts pollinators!

Shrub for Shade: Lily of the Valley Bush

This stunning harbinger of early spring, also known as pieris, showcases draping blossoms reminiscent of the perennial lily of the valley. Its shiny evergreen foliage provides year-round garden allure and structure, making it a unique flowering shrub that thrives in partial shade.

Its alluring blossoms, year-round foliage, and adaptability to partial shade make it a unique and enchanting addition to any garden. This perennial beauty, with its graceful presence, reminds us of the exquisite wonders that nature brings, offering a slice of spring's promise throughout the year.

Fast-Growing Flowering Shrub: Forsythia

When the forsythia blossoms, it serves as a reliable indicator that the arrival of spring is imminent. Seek out modern varieties that exhibit a more compact and well-mannered growth pattern compared to traditional ones, which may become excessively tall and cumbersome in limited garden spaces. These can be planted as striking accents or incorporated into mixed borders.

During early spring, vibrant canary yellow blooms emerge on the shrub before the leaves make their appearance. Throughout the remainder of the year, it maintains an attractive deciduous form. Moreover, it is a rapid-growing plant!
